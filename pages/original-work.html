Green AI Optimization Framework

A comprehensive approach to measuring and reducing the carbon footprint of machine learning models

Python Machine Learning Sustainability Data Science

Project Overview

The Green AI Optimization Framework is my ISM original workâ€”a practical toolkit designed to help developers and researchers understand and reduce the environmental impact of their AI projects. This project combines research findings with practical tools to make green AI practices accessible and actionable.

Key Components

Energy Monitor

A Python library that tracks energy consumption during model training and inference, providing real-time metrics and historical analysis.

Carbon Calculator

Converts energy usage to carbon emissions based on regional grid data, helping developers understand the true environmental cost of their work.

Efficiency Analyzer

Analyzes model architecture and suggests optimizations for improved energy efficiency without significant performance loss.

Best Practices Guide

Documentation compiling green AI practices from research literature and industry experience, with practical implementation guidance.

Technical Approach

The framework is built on several key technical principles:

  • Minimal Overhead: Monitoring tools are designed to add less than 2% overhead to training time
  • Cross-Platform Support: Compatible with major ML frameworks including PyTorch and TensorFlow
  • Accurate Measurement: Uses hardware-level power measurement when available, with validated software estimation as fallback
  • Actionable Insights: Reports include specific recommendations for reducing environmental impact

Research Foundation

This project is grounded in academic research on green AI, drawing from papers on energy-efficient computing, carbon-aware scheduling, and sustainable machine learning practices. Key influences include work from institutions like MIT, Stanford, and the University of Massachusetts Amherst.
