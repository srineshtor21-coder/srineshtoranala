Research Articles

A curated collection of academic articles, papers, and publications that inform my understanding of Green AI and sustainable computing.

Energy-Efficient Machine Learning

This collection explores cutting-edge techniques for reducing the energy consumption of ML model training and inference, including neural architecture search optimized for efficiency, pruning methods, and quantization strategies.

Machine Learning 5 papers reviewed

Key Takeaways:

  • Sparse neural networks can reduce computation by up to 90% with minimal accuracy loss
  • Quantization from 32-bit to 8-bit integers can provide 4x speedup with less than 1% accuracy drop
  • Knowledge distillation enables smaller models to match larger model performance

Carbon-Aware Computing

Research on how computational workloads can be scheduled to minimize carbon emissions by taking advantage of variations in grid carbon intensity and renewable energy availability.

Sustainable Computing 4 papers reviewed

Key Takeaways:

  • Grid carbon intensity varies by up to 10x throughout the day
  • Geographic shifting of workloads can reduce emissions by 30-40%
  • Real-time carbon signals enable dynamic workload optimization

Sustainable AI Infrastructure

Examining the role of green data centers, renewable energy integration, and efficient hardware in creating sustainable infrastructure for AI computation.

Infrastructure 6 papers reviewed

Key Takeaways:

  • Power Usage Effectiveness (PUE) of leading data centers has improved to 1.1-1.2
  • Liquid cooling can reduce cooling energy by up to 50%
  • On-site renewable energy generation is becoming cost-competitive

AI for Environmental Applications

Papers exploring how AI itself can be used to address environmental challenges, from climate modeling to wildlife conservation and sustainable agriculture.

Applications 7 papers reviewed

Key Takeaways:

  • ML models improve weather prediction accuracy by 15-20%
  • Computer vision enables automated monitoring of ecosystems
  • Optimization algorithms reduce agricultural resource usage by 25%
